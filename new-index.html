Exploring the Origins of Planets with Machine Learning and Cutting-Edge Observations

The Millennium Nucleus on Young Exoplanets and their Moons (YEMS) is a multidisciplinary research center uniting astronomers, informatics engineers and data scientists from four leading Chilean universities: UDP, USACH, PUC, and UdeC. Funded by ANID-Iniciativa Científica Milenio, our mission is to detect and characterize young exoplanets and exomoons—shedding light on how these distant worlds form and evolve.

Our collaborative network includes Astro UDP, Fisica USACH, CIRAS, Planetario Chile, and Astrodiálogos. Dive into our science and discover how we’re advancing the frontier of planetary origins.

Our Science

At YEMS, we explore how planets and moons form—and what makes them so diverse. Using powerful telescopes and cutting-edge AI tools, we study the cradles of distant worlds to understand their origins and search for signs of habitability. From discovering young exoplanets to developing techniques that sharpens our cosmic view, YEMS is helping unlock the secrets of planetary systems beyond our own.

Research Highlights and Publications

YEMS has established itself as a leader in the study of planet and moon formation, with over 80 peer-reviewed publications to date (July 2025). Our team has made landmark contributions, including the first observational evidence of planet formation via gravitational instability (Weber et al., ApJL), highlighted by ESO and ALMA press releases. YEMS researchers have played key roles in high-profile discoveries such as the exoplanet AF Lep b, the detection of water in a planet-forming disk, and the streamer in the eruptive star FU Ori. We are advancing the frontier of exomoon detection, with students and postdocs sharing results at international conferences in astronomy, astroinformatics, and astronomy education.
